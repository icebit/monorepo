# Pantry Staples

Things to keep stocked at all times. If these are in the house, a decent meal
is always 20 minutes away regardless of whether a grocery trip happened that
week.

## Dry Goods (long shelf life)
- Red lentils
- Green/brown lentils
- Rolled oats
- Brown rice
- Quinoa
- Whole wheat wraps/roti
- Chia seeds
- Flax seeds (ground)
- Protein powder (plant-based or whey isolate — low sat fat)
- Nuts: almonds, walnuts, cashews

## Canned / Jarred
- Chickpeas (several cans)
- Black beans
- Kidney beans
- Canned diced tomatoes
- Light coconut milk
- Canned tuna (in water or olive oil)
- Tahini
- Almond butter

## Frozen
- Frozen spinach (backup for smoothies and palak dal)
- Frozen mixed berries
- Frozen mango chunks
- Frozen bananas (peel and freeze ripe ones)
- Frozen whole wheat naan
- Frozen edamame
- Frozen mixed vegetables (stir-fry blend, broccoli, etc.)

## Oils / Condiments
- Extra virgin olive oil (the default cooking fat)
- Soy sauce or tamari
- Hot sauce
- Lemon juice (bottled is fine as backup)
- Rice vinegar
- Maple syrup (small amount, for oats/smoothies)

## Spices
- Cumin (ground and whole seeds)
- Coriander (ground)
- Turmeric
- Garam masala
- Kashmiri chili powder (or paprika as substitute)
- Black mustard seeds
- Cardamom pods
- Cinnamon
- Smoked paprika
- Black pepper
- Red chili flakes
- Salt

## Fresh (buy weekly)
- Onions
- Garlic
- Ginger
- Lemons/limes
- Cilantro
- Spinach/kale (for smoothies and cooking)
- Bananas
- Seasonal vegetables for roasting (rotate: broccoli, sweet potato,
  zucchini, bell pepper, cauliflower)
- Oat milk

## Proteins (buy weekly)
- Chicken thighs (skinless, boneless)
- Salmon fillets or other fish
- Firm tofu
- Eggs

## Notes

- The dry/canned/frozen/spice categories are the safety net. Stock these in
  one Costco-style trip and they last weeks to months.
- Fresh and protein categories are the weekly buy. If nothing else, get
  onions, garlic, spinach, oat milk, and one protein.
- If the pantry is stocked, the "emergency meals" (dal + naan, loaded sweet
  potato, bean scramble) are always available with zero planning.
