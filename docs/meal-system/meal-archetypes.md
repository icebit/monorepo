# Meal Archetypes

A starting roster of meals that fit the philosophy. Each is tagged with
attributes that the app will use for planning and filtering.

Tags:
- **prep**: time to make from scratch (not counting passive simmering/baking)
- **batch**: can it be made in bulk and stored for 3-5 days?
- **portable**: works as an office lunch (room temp or microwaved)?
- **one-pot**: minimal dishes?
- **category**: smoothie | breakfast | lunch | dinner | flexible

---

## Smoothies

The daily anchor. 5 minutes, one blender, massive nutrient density.

### 1. Green Power Smoothie
Spinach, frozen banana, frozen mango, flax seeds, oat milk, scoop of protein
powder. Add chia seeds if stocked.
- prep: 5 min | batch: no (blend daily) | portable: yes (in a bottle) | one-pot: yes
- category: smoothie
- notes: the default. rotate greens (kale, spinach) to vary nutrients.

### 2. Berry Oat Smoothie
Frozen mixed berries, rolled oats, almond butter (1 tbsp), oat milk, protein
powder, handful of spinach.
- prep: 5 min | batch: no | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: smoothie
- notes: oats add soluble fiber (cholesterol benefit). almond butter keeps
  it filling.

### 3. Tropical Anti-Inflammatory
Frozen pineapple, turmeric (fresh or powder), ginger, coconut water, flax,
spinach, protein powder.
- prep: 5 min | batch: no | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: smoothie
- notes: turmeric + ginger for anti-inflammatory. use black pepper to
  activate curcumin.

## Indian Mains

The heart of the rotation. Most of these are naturally batch-friendly,
legume-heavy, and low in saturated fat.

### 4. Red Lentil Dal (Masoor Dal)
Red lentils, onion, tomato, garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, coriander,
finished with a light olive oil tadka instead of ghee.
- prep: 15 min | batch: yes (freezes well) | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: the workhorse meal. dead simple, extremely cheap, high in soluble
  fiber. pair with brown rice or whole wheat roti.

### 5. Chana Masala
Canned or cooked chickpeas, onion, tomato, garam masala, cumin, coriander,
chili. Finished with lemon juice and cilantro.
- prep: 20 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: chickpeas are a fiber and protein powerhouse. use canned to cut
  prep time without sacrificing much.

### 6. Chicken Rogan Josh (Lean)
Chicken thighs (skinless) substituted for lamb, yogurt-based marinade (use
low-fat yogurt), onion, tomato, kashmiri chili, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves.
- prep: 30 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: dinner
- notes: traditional rogan josh uses lamb which is high in saturated fat.
  chicken version keeps the flavor profile with a fraction of the sat fat.
  can do lamb occasionally as a treat.

### 7. Palak Dal (Spinach Lentils)
Moong dal or red lentils cooked with garlic, cumin, then wilted spinach
stirred in. Light tadka with olive oil, mustard seeds, dried chili.
- prep: 20 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: combines the lentil base with a full serving of greens. very
  forgiving recipe.

### 8. Rajma (Kidney Bean Curry)
Kidney beans (canned or pressure-cooked), onion, tomato, ginger-garlic,
garam masala. Thick, hearty gravy.
- prep: 20 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: another soluble fiber bomb. comfort food tier. pairs with rice.

### 9. Tofu Tikka Masala
Baked or pan-fried tofu in a tomato-cashew cream sauce (blended cashews
instead of heavy cream), onion, garam masala, kashmiri chili, fenugreek.
- prep: 30 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: no (two steps)
- category: dinner
- notes: cashew cream gives richness without dairy. can batch the sauce
  and the tofu separately.

## Mediterranean / Bowl Meals

Simple assembly-style meals. Great for lunches because many work at room
temp.

### 10. Grain Bowl (Base Template)
Base: quinoa, brown rice, or farro. Top with: roasted vegetables (whatever
is on hand), chickpeas or white beans, handful of greens, tahini dressing
or lemon-olive oil. Optional: feta crumbles (small amount).
- prep: 15 min (if grain is pre-cooked) | batch: yes (prep components) | portable: yes | one-pot: no
- category: lunch
- notes: the modular lunch. cook a big batch of grain on the weekend, roast
  a sheet pan of vegetables, assemble daily in 5 minutes.

### 11. Lentil Soup (Mediterranean Style)
Green or brown lentils, carrot, celery, onion, garlic, cumin, lemon juice,
olive oil. Optional: smoked paprika.
- prep: 15 min | batch: yes (freezes great) | portable: yes (thermos) | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: different flavor profile from Indian dal but same nutritional
  punch. good variety to keep lentils from getting monotonous.

### 12. Shakshuka
Canned tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, garlic, cumin, paprika, chili flakes.
Poach eggs directly in the sauce.
- prep: 20 min | batch: sauce yes, eggs no | portable: no | one-pot: yes
- category: breakfast/dinner
- notes: good dinner option when you want something quick and different.
  the eggs are low in sat fat and high in protein.

### 13. Hummus Wrap
Whole wheat wrap, thick layer of hummus, cucumber, tomato, pickled onion,
greens, maybe some grilled chicken or falafel.
- prep: 5 min (if components exist) | batch: no | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: lunch
- notes: ultimate low-friction portable lunch. keep hummus stocked and this
  is always available.

## Fish

Two times a week target for omega-3s (actively beneficial for cholesterol).

### 14. Baked Salmon with Roasted Veg
Salmon fillet, olive oil, lemon, garlic. Sheet pan with whatever vegetables:
broccoli, sweet potato, bell pepper, zucchini.
- prep: 10 min | batch: no (best fresh) | portable: yes (leftovers) | one-pot: yes (sheet pan)
- category: dinner
- notes: sheet pan = one dish to clean. omega-3s from salmon directly
  improve lipid profiles.

### 15. Fish Curry (Goan Style)
White fish or shrimp in a coconut milk sauce (use light coconut milk),
tomato, onion, tamarind, turmeric, chili.
- prep: 25 min | batch: yes | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: dinner
- notes: light coconut milk keeps sat fat reasonable. tamarind gives the
  signature sour note.

### 16. Tuna/Salmon Grain Bowl
Canned tuna or leftover salmon over grain base, edamame, cucumber, avocado,
sesame seeds, soy-ginger dressing.
- prep: 10 min | batch: no | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: lunch
- notes: good way to use leftover fish. avocado adds healthy fats.

## Quick / Low-Friction

For days when energy is zero but you still want to eat within the philosophy.

### 17. Overnight Oats
Rolled oats, chia seeds, oat milk, dash of maple syrup. Top in morning with
banana, berries, nuts.
- prep: 5 min (night before) | batch: yes (make 3 jars at once) | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: breakfast
- notes: soluble fiber from oats. make 3 jars Sunday night, grab one each
  morning. basically zero morning effort.

### 18. Bean and Grain Scramble
Canned black beans or chickpeas, leftover rice or quinoa, sauteed with
onion, cumin, chili flakes, squeeze of lime. Optional fried egg on top.
- prep: 10 min | batch: no | portable: yes | one-pot: yes
- category: flexible
- notes: the "fridge clean out" meal. works with almost any leftover grain
  and any canned bean.

### 19. Dal + Frozen Naan Emergency Meal
Leftover or freshly made simple dal, frozen whole wheat naan from the store.
Side of raw cucumber and tomato.
- prep: 5 min (if dal exists) | batch: n/a | portable: no | one-pot: yes
- category: dinner
- notes: the "I don't want to think" dinner. keep frozen naan stocked as
  an insurance policy.

### 20. Loaded Sweet Potato
Microwave a sweet potato. Top with canned black beans (heated), salsa,
avocado, squeeze of lime.
- prep: 10 min | batch: no | portable: no | one-pot: yes
- category: dinner
- notes: genuinely zero cooking skill required. surprisingly satisfying.
