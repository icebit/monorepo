# Exercise Philosophy

## Core Principle

Move consistently at moderate intensity. Optimize for decades, not PRs.
The goal is a body that works well at 40, 50, 60 — not one that looks good
on Instagram this month.

## Priorities (in order)

1. **Consistency over intensity.** 15 minutes done is better than 45 minutes
   skipped. The routine should be short enough that "I don't have time" is
   never true.
2. **Longevity and joint health.** No ego lifting, no grinding through pain.
   Controlled movements, full range of motion, adequate rest.
3. **Cardiovascular health.** Especially important given familial
   hypercholesterolemia. Regular cardio directly improves lipid profiles.
4. **Functional strength.** Compound movements that build real-world
   capability: squatting, hinging, pressing, pulling, carrying.
5. **Low friction.** Everything happens at home or in the neighborhood. No
   gym membership, no commute, no barrier to starting.

## Equipment Available

- 40 lb kettlebell
- 25 lb dumbbells (pair)
- Running shoes
- Bicycle

That's enough. Seriously. A kettlebell and a pair of dumbbells can build a
strong, functional body if the programming is consistent.

## Structure

3 days per week to start. Build to 4-5 if it sticks.

- **Day A: Strength (Upper focus)** — 15-20 min
- **Day B: Cardio (Run or Bike)** — 20-30 min, easy pace
- **Day C: Strength (Lower focus + core)** — 15-20 min

Alternate: A - B - rest - C - B - rest - rest. Or just do whichever is next
when you have the energy. The app will suggest, not mandate.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Skipping because you don't feel like the scheduled workout.** The app
  should let you swap — if legs feel heavy, do upper body. Something is
  always better than nothing.
- **Going too hard early.** Start lighter than you think. Build the habit
  first, then build intensity. You'll feel undertrained for the first 2
  weeks. That's correct.
- **All-or-nothing thinking.** Missed 3 days? The answer is to do 15 minutes
  today, not to "restart Monday."
