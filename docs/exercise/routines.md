# Exercise Routines

All routines are designed for 15-20 minutes with the available equipment
(40 lb kettlebell, 25 lb dumbbells). Each exercise includes rep guidance.
Rest 30-60 seconds between sets unless noted otherwise.

## Strength A: Upper Body Push/Pull

Focus: shoulders, chest, back, arms. All standing or floor — no bench needed.

1. **Kettlebell Halo** (warmup) — 8 each direction
   Hold kettlebell by the horns at chest height. Circle it around your head
   slowly, keeping core tight. This warms up shoulders and thoracic spine.

2. **Dumbbell Overhead Press** — 3 x 10
   Standing, press both dumbbells overhead. Lock out at top, controlled
   descent. Keep core braced and don't arch your lower back.

3. **Dumbbell Bent-Over Row** — 3 x 10 each arm
   Hinge forward at the hips, flat back. Pull dumbbell to your hip, squeeze
   shoulder blade at top. Slow on the way down.

4. **Kettlebell Floor Press** — 3 x 10 each arm
   Lie on floor, press kettlebell up from chest level. The floor limits your
   range of motion which is actually easier on the shoulders than a bench.

5. **Push-ups** — 3 x max (aim for 10-15)
   Full range of motion. Chest to floor, full lockout. If 10 is too hard,
   do them from knees — no shame, build up.

6. **Dumbbell Curl to Press** — 2 x 10
   Curl both dumbbells up, then press overhead in one fluid motion. Good
   finisher that hits biceps and shoulders.

## Strength B: Lower Body + Core

Focus: legs, glutes, core. Kettlebell-heavy day.

1. **Kettlebell Goblet Squat** (warmup) — 2 x 8, light and slow
   Hold kettlebell at chest by the horns. Squat deep, elbows inside knees
   at bottom. Focus on getting full depth.

2. **Kettlebell Deadlift** — 3 x 12
   Kettlebell on floor between feet. Hip hinge to grab it, drive hips
   forward to stand. Squeeze glutes at top. This is the foundation movement
   — learn this well.

3. **Dumbbell Reverse Lunge** — 3 x 10 each leg
   Hold dumbbells at sides. Step back into a lunge, knee just above floor.
   Drive through front foot to stand. Alternate legs.

4. **Kettlebell Swing** — 3 x 15
   The king of kettlebell exercises. Hip hinge, snap hips forward, let the
   bell float to chest height. Arms are just along for the ride — power
   comes from hips. Don't squat it, hinge it.

5. **Dead Bug** — 3 x 8 each side
   Lie on back, arms straight up, knees at 90 degrees. Extend opposite arm
   and leg slowly, keeping lower back pressed into floor. This trains deep
   core stability.

6. **Plank** — 2 x 30-45 seconds
   On elbows. Straight line from head to heels. Squeeze everything. If 45
   seconds is easy, go longer or add a small weight on your back.

## Strength C: Full Body (Minimal)

For days when you want to do something but don't have much energy. 12-15 min.

1. **Kettlebell Goblet Squat** — 3 x 10
2. **Push-ups** — 3 x max
3. **Kettlebell Swing** — 3 x 15
4. **Plank** — 2 x 30-45 seconds

Four exercises, hits everything. Good enough.

## Cardio: Easy Run

20-30 minutes. Conversational pace — you should be able to talk in full
sentences. If you're gasping, slow down. Walk uphills if needed.

No distance goal. No pace goal. Just time on feet. The cardiovascular benefit
comes from consistency, not intensity.

## Cardio: Easy Bike

20-30 minutes. Same principle as running — keep it conversational. Good
option when legs are sore from strength work since biking is lower impact.

## Quick Stretch (post-workout, 5 min)

Do this after any session:
1. **Hip flexor stretch** — 30 sec each side (lunge position, back knee down)
2. **Hamstring stretch** — 30 sec each side (foot on a step or chair)
3. **Chest doorway stretch** — 30 sec (arm against door frame, lean through)
4. **Cat-cow** — 10 reps (hands and knees, arch and round spine)
5. **Child's pose** — 30 sec (sit back on heels, arms extended)
